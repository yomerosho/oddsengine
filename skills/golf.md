# Golf Betting Skill (Reference)

## Core Concepts
- **Strokes Gained (SG)**: Best metric in golf — total + 
  category breakdown
  - SG: Off the Tee (driving)
  - SG: Approach (iron play)
  - SG: Around the Green (chipping/sand)
  - SG: Putting
- **Total SG = sum of all four**
- **Course history & course fit**: Some courses reward 
  bombers, others reward accuracy
- **Field strength varies by event**

## Tournament Format
- **4 days (72 holes)**, cut after 36 holes (top 65 + ties)
- **Friday afternoon tee times** matter — weather changes
- **Wave splits (AM/PM Thursday/Friday)** — wind/rain bias

## Prop Drivers
1. Course fit (driving distance vs. accuracy demands)
2. SG: Approach (most predictive of low rounds)
3. Putting on bermuda vs. bent vs. poa annua greens
4. Recent form (last 6 events)
5. Wave/draw bias (weather dependent)
6. Cut probability (separate skill from win probability)

## Key Situational Factors
- **Majors (Masters, PGA, US Open, Open Championship)**: 
  Sharp markets
- **WGC/Signature events**: Smaller fields (~70 players, 
  no cut)
- **Pro-Am events** (AT&T, Phoenix, etc.): Casual atmosphere
- **Weather**: Wind and rain favor different golfers
- **Major champions in majors**: Pedigree adjustment

## Common Markets
- **Outright winner** (longshot value, but hard hit)
- **Top 5 / Top 10 / Top 20 finish**
- **Make/Miss the cut**
- **Head-to-head matchups (3-ball, 2-ball)**
- **Round leader (specific round)**
- **First-round leader (FRL)**

## Edge Spots
- Course fit specialists undervalued at non-major events
- Make-the-cut props on consistent grinders (low variance)
- Head-to-head 3-balls — much sharper than outrights
- Top 20 props on top-30 ranked players (often soft)

## Caution
- **Outright winner odds**: Even best players win 5-10% 
  of starts — payoffs feel lottery-like
- **Field of 156** = high variance
- **Weather creates randomness** in specific waves
- **Limit yourself to majors + signature events** as a 
  beginner — smaller fields = better data
