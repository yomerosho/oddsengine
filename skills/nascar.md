# NASCAR Betting Skill (Reference)

## Core Concepts
- **Track types**:
  - **Superspeedway** (Daytona, Talladega): Drafting, 
    crash-fest variance
  - **Intermediate** (1.5-mile ovals): Most common, 
    aero/handling matter
  - **Short track** (Bristol, Martinsville): Beating and 
    banging
  - **Road course** (COTA, Watkins Glen, Sonoma): 
    Different skill set
- **Stage racing**: Points awarded at end of each stage
- **Playoff format**: 16 drivers, elimination rounds

## Prop Drivers
1. Track type fit (drivers specialize)
2. Recent form at this track
3. Equipment quality (Hendrick, Penske, JGR are top tier)
4. Stage points history
5. Crash variance (superspeedways especially)

## Key Situational Factors
- **Daytona 500 / All-Star Race**: Highest variance events
- **Playoff cutoff races**: Drivers desperate for points
- **Rain delays**: Affect tire strategy & late-race results
- **Cautions**: Reset gaps, change strategy
- **Stage strategy**: Some teams sacrifice stages for 
  long-run setup

## Common Markets
- **Race winner** (very high variance — 36-driver field)
- **Top 3 / Top 5 / Top 10 finish**
- **Head-to-head matchups (2-driver props)**
- **Stage winners**
- **Manufacturer winner** (Ford / Chevy / Toyota)

## Edge Spots
- Head-to-head matchups between drivers in similar 
  equipment (cleaner edge than outrights)
- Top 10 props on consistent finishers (no Daytona/Talladega)
- Manufacturer matchups when one OEM clearly dominates 
  current package

## Caution
- **Highest crash-variance major sport** — outright winners 
  are near-lottery
- **Equipment > driver** in many cases — Hendrick driver 
  in 30th place can win, top-5 driver in bad equipment can't
- **Stick to head-to-heads and Top 10 props** — outright 
  winners are -EV unless sharp model finds edge
- **Avoid superspeedway outrights entirely** — random 
  finishes (literally luck of the draw on the last lap)
