# NCAA Basketball Betting Skill (Reference)

## Core Metrics
- **KenPom ratings**: Adjusted offensive/defensive efficiency
- **Bart Torvik**: Similar to KenPom, often free
- **Offensive/Defensive efficiency per 100 possessions**
- **Tempo (possessions per game)**

## Key Differences from NBA
- **Possession count varies wildly** (60–80 per game)
- **Talent gap can be massive** (Power 6 vs. Mid-Major)
- **Conference play tightens** as teams adjust to each other
- **One-and-done stars**: Volatile freshman performances

## Prop Drivers
1. Tempo (KenPom adjusted)
2. Star player usage (in college, often 30%+)
3. Foul trouble risk (huge in college — 5 fouls = out)
4. Home court advantage (bigger than NBA — 3-4 points)
5. Travel / conference road trips

## Key Situational Factors
- **March Madness**: Sharp markets, public floods, 
  edges harder to find
- **Conference tournaments**: Fatigue from playing 3-4 
  games in 3 days
- **Senior Night / Rivalry games**: Emotional bumps
- **Bracketology season**: Late-season motivation

## Common Prop Types
- Player points, rebounds, assists
- 3PM (more variance than NBA)
- Game totals (tempo-driven)

## Information Sources
- **KenPom.com** (paid but essential for serious CBB)
- **Bart Torvik (barttorvik.com)** — free alternative
- **CBB Twitter**: @JeffBorzello, @JonRothstein
- **Lineups.com / Rotogrinders** for projections

## Edge Spots
- Tempo mismatches (top-10 pace vs. bottom-10 pace = total over)
- Star player from team that just lost a teammate (usage spike)
- Mid-major matchups books don't price sharply

## Caution
- Sample sizes are smaller (30-game seasons)
- Player props less liquid than NBA — markets thinner
- Avoid betting first 5 games of season (no sample yet)
