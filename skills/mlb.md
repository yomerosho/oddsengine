# MLB Betting Skill

## Core Metrics
- **xFIP / SIERA**: Pitcher metrics that strip out luck/defense
- **wOBA (Weighted On-Base Average)**: Batter total offensive value
- **wRC+**: Park-adjusted offensive value (100 = league average)
- **xwOBA**: Expected wOBA based on contact quality
- **Barrel %**: % of batted balls with optimal exit velo + launch angle
- **K% / BB%**: Strikeout & walk rates (better than counting stats)
- **CSW% (Called Strikes + Whiffs)**: Pitcher dominance metric

## Prop Drivers (in priority order)
1. **Starting pitcher matchup** — biggest single driver
2. **Park factors** — Coors vs. Petco are different sports
3. **Batter handedness vs. pitcher handedness** (BvP)
4. **Lineup spot** — top of order = more PAs
5. **Weather** — wind direction, temperature
6. **Bullpen fatigue** — recent usage drives late-game scoring

## Key Situational Factors
- **Park factors**:
  - Coors Field: Hitters' paradise (altitude)
  - Great American: HR-friendly
  - Petco / Oakland Coliseum: Pitcher-friendly
  - Yankee Stadium: Short porch (LH HR boost)
- **Weather**:
  - Wind blowing out: HR & total overs
  - Wind blowing in: Unders
  - Hot temps: Ball travels farther
  - Humidity: Affects ball flight
- **Day vs. night games** — afternoon games (especially 
  after night game) often see fatigued lineups
- **Travel** — west-to-east early games are tough
- **Catcher framing** — affects pitcher Ks
- **Umpire** — wide vs. tight strike zone

## Prop-Specific Notes

### Pitcher Strikeouts
- Best prop type for sharp analysis
- K% × expected batters faced (BF)
- Opponent K% vs. pitcher handedness
- Park doesn't matter much; matchup does
- Check if pitcher is on innings limit / pitch count

### Hits
- Lineup spot (1-3 = most PAs)
- Pitcher allowed contact rate
- Park factor secondary to matchup

### Total Bases
- HR-leverage prop — variant
- Hitter's barrel% vs. pitcher's HR-allowed rate
- Park + wind matter more here

### Home Runs
- Highest variance prop in baseball
- Don't bet without: hitter ISO + pitcher HR/9 + park + 
  wind alignment
- Better as DFS slip leg than standalone

### RBIs
- Highly dependent on teammates getting on base
- Lineup spot 3-5 = RBI opportunity peaks

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Betting before lineup confirmation (LH/RH matters huge)
- Ignoring park factors (Coors changes everything)
- Trusting BvP (Batter vs. Pitcher) small samples 
  (10 ABs is noise)
- Betting overs without checking wind direction
- Stacking same-game hitters in DFS without correlation 
  awareness
- Pitcher props on hot/dome days vs. same pitcher on cold 
  days (different)

## Edge Spots
- Strong K-pitcher vs. high-K opponent → K prop over
- Weak pitcher in hitter's park, wind out → game total over
- Lineup change moves star to leadoff → hits/runs over
- Pitcher returning from IL with low pitch count → 
  K under (innings cap)
- Day game after night game → lineup fatigue, team total under
- Bullpen game (no real starter) → heavy total over

## Season Calendar
- Spring Training (Feb-Mar): Avoid — meaningless
- April–September: Regular season (162 games)
- October: Playoffs (sharp markets, harder edges)
- All-Star Break: Mid-July, no games for 4 days
- Trade Deadline: July 31 — affects player roles

## Volume Warning
MLB has ~15 games/day. **Don't bet every slate.** Pick 1-3 
edges per day max. Volume kills bankroll.
