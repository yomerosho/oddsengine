# MMA Betting Skill (Reference)

## Core Concepts
- **Striking accuracy & defense** (UFC StatLeaders)
- **Significant strikes per minute (SLpM)**
- **Takedown accuracy & defense**
- **Submission attempts per 15 min**
- **Reach, height, age, fight IQ**

## Style Matchup Logic
- **Striker vs. striker**: Often goes to decision; bet on 
  output volume
- **Striker vs. grappler**: Wrestler usually wins if can 
  close distance; striker wins if they sprawl
- **Grappler vs. grappler**: Usually decision; check 
  cardio + scrambling ability
- **Cardio**: Round 3 onwards is where edges open up

## Prop Drivers
1. Style matchup (striker/wrestler/BJJ)
2. Reach and height differential
3. Cardio (especially in 5-rounders)
4. Age (35+ fighters decline noticeably)
5. Camp quality (where did they train?)
6. Layoff (long layoffs = ring rust risk)
7. Weight cut difficulty (bad cuts = compromised cardio)

## Key Situational Factors
- **Title fights (5 rounds)** vs. **prelims (3 rounds)** 
  — different math
- **Short notice fights**: Replacement is usually disadvantaged
- **Home country bias** for international cards
- **Travel/altitude**: Mexico City cards favor altitude-trained
- **Fight night vs. PPV**: Different roster quality

## Common Markets
- **Moneyline** (most common)
- **Method of victory** (KO/TKO, Submission, Decision)
- **Round betting** (which round it ends)
- **Total rounds (over/under 1.5 / 2.5)**
- **Goes to decision (Yes/No)**
- **Fight to start round X**

## Information Sources
- **UFC Stats (ufcstats.com)** — official
- **Tapology, Sherdog** — full fight history
- **MMA Twitter**: @arielhelwani, @bokamotoESPN
- **MMA Junkie / The Athletic MMA coverage**

## Edge Spots
- Heavy underdog grapplers vs. one-dimensional strikers 
  (recipe for upset)
- Method of victory props on KO artists in striking matchups 
  (often softer than ML)
- Fight goes to decision YES on two cardio-questionable strikers
- Round 1 finish props on aggressive openers (underrated)

## Caution
- **Highest variance major sport** — anything can happen
- **Bet sizing should be smaller than other sports** 
  (more like 0.5% units)
- **Avoid heavy favorites** (-400 or worse) — bad ROI even if right
- **Prelims are harder** — less data, fighters less consistent
