# NCAA Football Betting Skill (Reference)

## Core Metrics
- **SP+ (Bill Connelly)**: Best public predictive ranking
- **FEI**: Fremeau Efficiency Index
- **Success rate & explosiveness** (EPA-based)
- **Returning production**: Talent retention year-over-year

## Key Differences from NFL
- **Talent gap is huge** — top teams blow out bad teams
- **Spreads can be 30+** — totals matter more than sides 
  in mismatches
- **Coaching > talent** at lower levels
- **Backup QB risk**: One injury can flip a team's season
- **Rivalry games**: Throw analytics out (some)

## Prop Drivers
1. Talent gap (recruiting rankings, transfer portal)
2. Coaching matchup
3. Pace of play (Air Raid vs. Run-heavy)
4. Home/away (HFA bigger than NFL — 3-4 points)
5. Late-season motivation (bowl eligibility, playoff position)

## Key Situational Factors
- **Look-ahead spots**: Big rivalry next week
- **Letdown spots**: After massive upset win
- **Conference championship implications**: Late season weight
- **Bowl games**: Opt-outs kill props (NFL-bound players sit)
- **Weather**: Outdoor stadiums, Northern teams

## Information Sources
- **CFBStats, SP+ ratings, Football Outsiders**
- **247Sports / On3 (recruiting)**
- **CFB Twitter**: @ESPN_BillC, @PFF_College
- **InsideTheLeagues** (transfer portal news)

## Edge Spots
- Mid-major dogs in spots where books overcorrect for brand
- Team totals in mismatches (favorites cover ATS but go 
  under on team total when up 35-0 in 3rd)
- Backup QB starting (line lags)

## Caution
- Limited prop markets vs. NFL
- Information asymmetry — locals know more than national bettors
- Avoid emotional bets on alma mater / rival schools
